My Radio Story

A story about how I use my family’s radio.
1

My family has radios. We use them to talk to each other when we are in different places. Talking on the radio can help me feel close to my family.

2

To turn my radio on, I turn the knob. I hear a small click. Now my radio is on. The same knob makes the sound louder or softer. I turn it a little until the sound feels just right for me — not too loud. A grown-up can help me find a good sound level.

3

My radio has one channel for me. It is Channel __. First, I turn my radio to Channel __. Now my radio is ready.

4

Sometimes my radio is quiet. Sometimes it beeps. Sometimes it makes a soft “shhhh” sound. That sound is called static. The beeps and the static are okay. They do not mean anything is wrong. If my radio is too loud, I can turn it down. A grown-up can help me.

5

To talk, I press the button and hold it. While I hold the button, I talk. When I finish talking, I let the button go. When the button is up, I can hear other people. I talk, or I listen. I do one at a time.

6

On the radio, one person talks at a time. When someone else is talking, I wait and listen. When they are done, it is my turn. Waiting is part of using the radio. I can wait.

7

When I want to talk to someone, I press the button and say:

“This is (call sign). (name) calling name.”

Then I let the button go and I listen.

8

Every little while, I say my call sign again. My call sign is (call sign). Saying it is a rule that helps everyone know who is talking. A grown-up can remind me. That is okay.

9

When I want to listen and wait, I press the button and say:

“This is (call sign). (name), and I’ll be on the side.”

This means I am still here. I am listening, but I am not talking right now.

10

When I am all done, I press the button and say:

“This is (call sign). (name), and I’ll be clear.”

This means I am finished talking. Then I can put my radio down.

11

Sometimes I call and no one answers right away. That is okay. I can wait and try again later. Sometimes I say the words in a different order. That is okay too. I can try again. I am learning.

12

I know how to use my radio. I press the button to talk. I let go to listen. I wait for my turn. My radio helps me stay close to my family. I can do this.
